Australians Twelfe Apostels

The Twelve Apostles are one of the highlights of the legendary Great Ocean Road and are one of the most famous sights of the red continent.

They are limestone rocks standing in the sea. Sunrise and sunset make the rocks shine in the surf. As long as you can, you shouldn't miss this sight, as the rocks are made of soft rock, the erosion process progresses quickly and gradually brings this rock formation to collapse.

The Port of Apollo Bay

Apollo Bay is a small town that is not only a strategic stopover on the Great Ocean Road, it also offers some unique sights of its own.

One of them is the port. There you can, if you like, watch various majestic stingrays slide through the water all day long.
